Project Overview

This project demonstrates the relationship between Fourier Series and music by generating a melody using pure sine waves and analyzing it with the Fast Fourier Transform (FFT). Each musical note (Sa, Re, Ga, Ma, Pa, Dha) is created as a pure sine wave, and the complete melody is analyzed to reveal its frequency components.

Fourier Series Connection

Fourier Series Formula

f(t) = a₀ + Σ(aₙ·cos(nω₀t) + bₙ·sin(nω₀t))

Each note = A sine wave:
Note(t) = sin(2π × f × t)

Where f is the frequency (e.g., Sa = 261.63 Hz)

FFT Implementation

X(k) = Σ x(n)·e-j2πkn/N

MATLAB Code:
Y = fft(song);
magnitude = abs(Y)/N;

The FFT computes the Discrete Fourier Transform of the sampled signal.

Complete Main Script (main.m)
%% 🎵 Fourier Music Analysis - Main Script
% This script demonstrates the relationship between Fourier Series and
% music by generating a melody and analyzing it with FFT.

clc; clear; close all;

%% Step 1: Setup Parameters
Fs = 8000; % Sampling frequency (Hz)
duration = 0.3; % Duration of each note (seconds)
t = 0:1/Fs:duration-1/Fs; % Time vector

%% Step 2: Define Musical Notes
Sa = 261.63; % C
Re = 293.66; % D
Ga = 329.63; % E
Ma = 349.23; % F
Pa = 392.00; % G
Dha = 440.00; % A

%% Step 3: Create Melody
melody = [Sa Sa Pa Pa Dha Dha Pa ...
Ma Ma Ga Ga Re Re Sa ...
Pa Pa Ma Ma Ga Ga Re ...
Pa Pa Ma Ma Ga Ga Re ...
Sa Sa Pa Pa Dha Dha Pa ...
Ma Ma Ga Ga Re Re Sa];

%% Step 4: Generate Audio Signal
song = [];
for i = 1:length(melody)
note = sin(2*pi*melody(i)*t);
song = [song note];
end
song = song / max(abs(song)); % Normalize

%% Step 5: Play and Save Audio
sound(song, Fs);
audiowrite('Twinkle_Twinkle_SaReGaMa.wav', song, Fs);

%% Step 6: FFT Analysis
N = length(song);
Y = fft(song);
f = (0:N-1)*(Fs/N);
magnitude = abs(Y)/N;

%% Step 7: Plot Frequency Spectrum
figure;
plot(f(1:N/2), magnitude(1:N/2));
xlabel('Frequency (Hz)');
ylabel('Magnitude');
title('FFT of Twinkle Twinkle Little Star');
grid on;

%% Step 8: Find Dominant Frequencies
[~, index] = sort(magnitude(1:N/2), 'descend');
dominant_frequencies = f(index(1:10));
disp('Dominant Frequencies:');
disp(dominant_frequencies);

Viva / Interview Preparation
❓ What is Fourier Series? Fourier Series states that any periodic signal can be expressed as a sum of sine and cosine waves with different frequencies and amplitudes.
❓ How does this project use Fourier Series? Each musical note is generated as a sine wave (sin(2π×f×t)). The complete melody is a combination of these waves. FFT breaks the melody back into its frequency components, showing the original notes.
❓ Why FFT instead of Fourier Series? Our melody is finite (not infinitely periodic) and digitally sampled. Fourier Series requires periodic signals, so we use the Discrete Fourier Transform (implemented as FFT) for our digital signal.
❓ What do the FFT peaks represent? Each peak corresponds to a musical note frequency: Sa (261.63 Hz), Re (293.66 Hz), Ga (329.63 Hz), Ma (349.23 Hz), Pa (392.00 Hz), and Dha (440.00 Hz). Higher peaks mean the note was played more times.
❓ What is the difference between Fourier Series and Fourier Transform? Fourier Series is for periodic signals and gives discrete frequency components. Fourier Transform is for non-periodic signals and gives a continuous frequency spectrum. We use FFT (Fast Fourier Transform) which is the computational implementation of the Discrete Fourier Transform.
❓ How is the signal normalized and why? The signal is normalized using song = song / max(abs(song)) to scale all values between -1 and 1. This prevents audio clipping when playing the sound and maintains consistent volume.
